Who We Are

Not a commune.
A founding group.

Desert Clam is a multi-generational community of skilled, intentional people — builders, healers, designers, farmers, artists, and parents — who have found each other and are now looking for the land that deserves what we bring.

We are neurodivergent, health-conscious, and deeply committed to building systems that regenerate rather than extract. We do not need to be managed. We need land, partnership, and legal clarity. Everything else we already have.

Between us we hold construction engineering, holistic and medical knowledge, permaculture design, community architecture, regenerative agriculture, cooking, animal husbandry, and creative arts. We arrive with tools, a blueprint, a constitution, and the critical mass to begin on day one.

What We Build

A living system,
not a housing arrangement

Every structure we build serves multiple functions. Every output feeds the next input. Nothing is decorative that is not also functional. Nothing is functional that is not also beautiful.

  • Staple crops: corn, bean, squash, potato, wheat, amaranth
  • Fruit and nut canopy: walnut, serviceberry, fig, currant

  • Aquaponics: fish tanks feeding herb growbeds
  • Greenhouse hydroponics: microgreens, herbs, year-round greens
  • Chinampa floating garden rafts on ponds
  • Hugelkultur beds as underground water reservoirs

  • Communal kitchen: stone, clay, juniper, wood-fired oven
  • Water harvesting: swales, crescent pools, cisterns

For the Landowner

What we offer the person
who has the land

"You have land you love and cannot develop alone. We have the people, the skills, the blueprint, and the intention. Together we build something neither of us could build separately — and your land becomes more alive than you imagined."
  • Long-term lease at below-market rate in exchange for all improvements
  • Every structure built increases your property value permanently
  • Food forest matures over decades — a gift to the land itself
  • Water systems improve the land's hydrology and fire resilience
  • A living, productive, beautiful property instead of vacant acreage
  • Formal written agreement — clear terms, legal protection for both sides
  • Pathway for community members to purchase small individual parcels over time
  • You are not selling. You are partnering.
  • Your land continues under stewardship that cares for it as you do
  • Revenue from the community partially offsets your property taxes

The right landowner is not primarily motivated by cash return. They have land they love and want to see tended, developed, and made beautiful by people who will treat it as their own — because it will become their own.

The Land We Seek

Specific. Non-negotiable.
Honest.

We have learned — directly and recently — what happens when foundations are unclear. We are not flexible on these criteria. We are honest about them so the right landowner finds us and the wrong one does not.

🏔 Climate

High desert or mountain. Dry. Low mold risk. Four seasons. We have members recovering from wildfire toxins and mold exposure — this is medical, not preferential.

💧 Water

Clean well or water rights. Tested and documented. No known uranium, arsenic, PFAS, or industrial contamination. We will test before committing.

📐 Acreage

20 acres minimum. Enough for food forest, paddocks, individual private areas, communal structures, and expansion. We are building a system, not a garden.

⚖️ Political

Progressive or politically neutral state law. We are a diverse, neurodivergent, health-focused community. State-level policy affects our daily safety and legal standing.

🔆 Solar Access

South-facing exposure for passive solar structures and solar array. We build off-grid. Sun is our utility.

📋 Legal Clarity

Clear title. Landowner has legal authority to enter agreements. We require attorney-reviewed documentation before breaking ground on anything permanent.

States we are open to

New Mexico Colorado Northern Arizona Eastern Oregon Eastern Washington Montana Nevada (selected areas)

Priority: New Mexico and Colorado. Clean water, dry climate, politically livable, strong existing permaculture networks, reasonable land prices.
