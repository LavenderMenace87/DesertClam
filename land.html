The Land We Seek

Specific. Non-negotiable. Honest. We have learned what happens when foundations are unclear.

Required Criteria

What the land must be

🏔 Climate Non-negotiable

High desert or mountain. Dry. Four seasons with real winter. Low humidity year-round. Several members are recovering from mold and wildfire toxin exposure — this requirement is medical, not aesthetic.

💧 Water Non-negotiable

Clean well or documented water rights. Tested for uranium, arsenic, PFAS, nitrates, and industrial contamination. We will conduct independent testing before committing to any property. No exceptions.

📐 Acreage 20 acres minimum

Sufficient for food forest, paddocks, individual private residence areas, communal structures, animal operations, and future expansion. We are building a system. Systems need space to breathe.

⚖️ Political Environment Non-negotiable

Progressive or politically neutral at the state level. We are a diverse, neurodivergent community with health-focused and non-traditional living arrangements. State law governs daily life in ways that matter to us.

☀️ Solar Access Required

South-facing exposure for passive solar earth structures and off-grid solar array. We build without grid dependency. Sun is our utility.

📋 Legal Clarity Non-negotiable

Clear title. Landowner has full legal authority to enter long-term agreements. All agreements reviewed by licensed attorney before any permanent structure is built. We will not break ground on a handshake.

What we will not consider
  • Rented land — the landowner must hold clear title
  • Humid climates with documented mold risk
  • States with hostile policy toward diverse or non-traditional communities
  • Properties with untested or known water contamination
  • Informal arrangements without written legal documentation
  • Situations where we cannot independently verify representations made about the property

The Partnership Structure

What a partnership
with Desert Clam looks like

We are not renters. We are not employees. We are a community that builds, improves, and stewards land in exchange for the right to occupy it and — over time — to own our small piece of it.

Proposed Term Sheet
TermOur Proposal
Occupancy AgreementLong-term lease 20–30 years, recorded with county, reviewed by attorney. Below-market rent in exchange for improvements.
Improvements OwnershipCommunity members retain ownership of structures they fund and build. Right to remove or sell to landowner at appraised value upon departure.
Property ValueEvery food forest, water system, and structure built increases the landowner's property value permanently. This is the return on the lease discount.
Individual Parcel PathwayCommunity members may purchase small individual parcels over time as community revenue develops. Landowner is not selling the land — specific small parcels become available by agreement as thresholds are reached.
Right of First RefusalCommunity holds right of first refusal if landowner sells. 60–90 days to match any offer.
Revenue SharingA portion of community revenue offsets landowner property tax burden. Specific percentage defined in agreement.
Land StewardshipCommunity commits to documented regenerative practices. Annual land health report provided to landowner.
Legal ReviewAll terms reviewed by attorney licensed in the relevant state before any permanent structure is built. Non-negotiable.
Dissolution TermsClear exit terms, notice period, asset disposition, and community members' rights defined in advance — not when things get complicated.

The right landowner is not primarily motivated by cash return. They have land they love, cannot develop alone, and want to see tended, built upon, and made beautiful by people who will treat it as their own — because it will become their own.
