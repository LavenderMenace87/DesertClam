The Covenant
Desert Clam Community Constitution · 2026

WHEREAS, we the founders and members of Desert Clam recognize that land is held in stewardship rather than dominion, and that a community flourishes through shared purpose, honest labor, beauty, and regeneration;

WHEREAS, Desert Clam exists as a regenerative healing community and creative land lab rooted in the high desert West;

NOW THEREFORE, we establish this Constitution as our governing charter.

Article I
Identity and Purpose
1.1Desert Clam is a private intentional community organized for regenerative living, holistic health, ecological stewardship, creative work, education, and mutually beneficial community life.
1.2The Community shall prioritize closed-loop systems, clean food, soil building, water harvesting, beauty in design, and practices that strengthen the land and the people on it.
1.3No interpretation of this Constitution shall permit extraction, neglect, toxic materials, or conduct inconsistent with the Community's regenerative purpose.
1.4This Constitution is designed as a replicable social contract — a case study and template for intentional communities that wish to build something similar.
Article II
Founding Values
These are not aspirational statements. They are the standard against which every decision, structure, and member is evaluated.
Creativity

The way you solve problems, build systems, imagine new forms. If you are here, you are making something.

Growth

Personal, ecological, communal. Things that stop growing, die.

Land Stewardship

You tend this land. You return more than you take. Every practice is evaluated against whether it regenerates or extracts.

Beauty

A standard of craft. The evidence that something was made with intention. Not decoration — commitment.

Responsibility

You show up for your commitments. To the land, to the people, to the agreements. This is the mechanism that makes the other four sustainable.

Article III
The Land
3.1The Community shall be situated on land that is dry, high-elevation, tested for water quality, legally clear, and accessible under a long-term recorded agreement.
3.2The wash corridor, water-harvesting features, and ecological systems of the property shall be treated as essential commons and protected accordingly.
3.3No harmful chemicals, no plastics where avoidable, no electricity dependency without built-in redundancy. Materials preference: clay, stone, timber, earthbag, adobe, cob.
3.4The Community shall comply with all applicable water laws and permitting requirements before constructing ponds, swales, or surface water features.
Article IV
Membership
4.1Membership is a privilege conditioned on adherence to this Constitution and the Community's values.
4.2Classes: Founding Members, Permanent Residents, Long-Stay Members, Working Guests, Rotating Volunteers, Retreat Guests.
4.3Permanent Residents commit to multi-year tenure, substantial labor, and participation in governance and commons maintenance.
4.4No person shall be admitted or retained who cannot live in good faith with the Community's values and agreements.
Article V
Governance
Personal domain is sovereign. The commons are governed collectively. The distinction between these is explicit, not assumed.
5.1Decisions shall be made as close as possible to the people most affected by them.
5.2Matters affecting private structures, personal practice, and individual conduct remain within the domain of the responsible person.
5.3Matters affecting commons — water systems, shared kitchen, land corridors, animal operations, community revenue — are governed collectively by voting members.
5.4No steward or founder shall exercise authority beyond the scope expressly granted by this Constitution.
Article VI
The Founders
6.1The Founding Steward holds the community vision and architecture. First among equals with founding authority. Not above the governance structure they created.
6.2All founder contributions — land, labor, capital, medical expertise, construction capacity — shall be defined in a written agreement reviewed by counsel licensed in the relevant state.
6.3No permanent structure shall be built before the founders' agreement is signed. This is not flexible.
Article VII
Land Use
7.1The land shall be used to increase fertility, conserve water, reduce fire risk, support habitat, and improve long-term productivity and beauty.
7.2Members shall not engage in extractive use without corresponding replenishment. This is the foundational law.
7.3The Community may designate conservation, production, residence, hospitality, and work zones by written plan, revisable by Community vote.
Article VIII
Housing and Structures
8.1Structures shall be durable, beautiful, climate-appropriate, low-impact, and capable of multiple functions.
8.2Each permanent resident selects their own private site. It is mapped, recorded, and protected. No one else builds there.
8.3Agreements about improvements shall specify ownership, removal rights, reimbursement rights, and treatment upon departure.
Article IX
Work and Contribution
This is not a passive residence. Membership carries obligations of labor proportionate to role and capacity.
9.1Each resident contributes to the Community: animal care, building, food production, cleaning, hospitality, administration, or education.
9.2The Community may establish labor-credit, revenue-share, housing-for-work, and barter arrangements by written agreement.
9.3Labor expectations shall be stated in advance and shall not be exceeded without consent.
Article X
Commons and Revenue
10.1Community income shall be accounted for transparently and used first for operating expenses, reserves, infrastructure, and agreed distributions.
10.2Where labor entitles compensation or revenue share, the formula shall be stated in writing and administered in good faith.
10.3The Community shall maintain bookkeeping sufficient to show income, expenses, reserves, and distributions with reasonable clarity.
Article XI
Review and Replication
11.1Annual Review. Every year the community gathers to reaffirm, revise, or sunset agreements. The living community governs — not the founders' original vision frozen in clay.
11.2Sunset Provisions. Rules not actively reaffirmed expire. No provision persists without consent of the people living under it.
11.3Conflict Resolution. Disputes go first to direct conversation, then to a circle of three — one chosen by each party, one chosen by both. Advisory, not binding, but carries weight.
11.4Replication. This Constitution is designed to be adapted. It is a case study and a template. The teaching mission of Desert Clam includes sharing this governance architecture with communities that want to build something similar.
